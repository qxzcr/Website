Tokyo

東京都

A trip to Tokyo is an immersion into modern metropolitan culture, where traditional temples coexist with towering skyscrapers and technological innovations. We can enjoy exquisite Japanese cuisine, explore fashionable districts like Harajuku and Shibuya, visit traditional Japanese gardens, and catch a glimpse of the majestic Mount Fuji. Tokyo is a place where traditions blend with modernity, creating a unique travel experience.

Kyoto

京都市

Kyoto is a city located in the Kansai region of Japan, known for its historical significance, cultural heritage, and beautiful scenery. It served as the imperial capital of Japan for over a thousand years, from 794 until 1868. The city is famous for its well-preserved temples, traditional wooden houses, and beautiful gardens.

Osaka

大阪市

Osaka is a major city in the Kansai region of Japan, known for its vibrant culture, delicious street food, and modern architecture. It's often referred to as the "Kitchen of Japan" due to its rich culinary scene. The city has a long history as a merchant city and was a crucial hub for trade during the Edo period.

Nara

平城京

Nara is a city located in the Kansai region of Japan, just south of Kyoto and Osaka. It holds significant historical and cultural importance as it was once the capital of Japan before Kyoto, from 710 to 784 AD, during the Nara period.

Fukuoka

福岡県

Fukuoka is a vibrant and dynamic city located on the northern shore of the island of Kyushu, Japan's southernmost main island. It's the largest city in Kyushu and serves as the region's economic and cultural hub. Fukuoka is known for its modernity, rich history, delicious food, and friendly atmosphere.
