City Guide
Fukuoka

Fukuoka is a vibrant city located on the northern shore of the island of Kyushu in Japan.
It's known for its rich history, bustling shopping districts, delicious food, and beautiful parks.
As one of Japan's largest cities, Fukuoka offers a diverse range of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy.

Fukuoka Towers

Standing at 234 meters tall, Fukuoka Tower is the tallest seaside tower in Japan. Visitors can take an elevator ride to the observation deck located 123 meters above ground level to enjoy stunning views of the city, Hakata Bay, and even as far as the Genkai Sea on clear days.

Canal City Hakata

Canal City Hakata in Fukuoka is a dynamic shopping and entertainment complex featuring a wide array of shops, restaurants, entertainment options, and a picturesque canal. It's a bustling hub where visitors can shop, dine, enjoy entertainment, and take in the scenic waterfront views.

Ohori Park

Ohori Park, situated in the heart of Fukuoka, boasts a scenic landscape with walking paths, bridges, and gardens, perfect for leisurely strolls and picnics. Visitors can explore the park's historic features, including remnants of a former castle moat, traditional tea houses, and stone lanterns. Additionally, the park hosts various events throughout the year, such as cherry blossom festivals and cultural performances, adding to its charm and appeal.

Fukuoka Castle

Fukuoka Castle, also known as Maizuru Castle, is a historic site located in Fukuoka City. Originally built in the early 17th century, the castle played a significant role in Japan's feudal history. While much of the original structure was destroyed over the years, visitors can still explore the castle grounds, which include reconstructed walls, gates, and watchtowers. The surrounding Maizuru Park offers picturesque views of the castle and the city skyline, making it a popular destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.
